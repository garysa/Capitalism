# Financial Capitalism as Structured Wealth Extraction

## The Core Argument

Financial capitalism is not a neutral system of capital allocation. Traced
mechanically — following the money flows without ideological framing — it
functions as a structured wealth extraction system that transfers value from
producers and workers to those who control the financial architecture.

This is not a fringe position. It is what the mechanics show.

---

## The Extraction Architecture — Step by Step

### Step 1 — Monopolise Money Creation

- Banks create money through lending — not metaphor, accounting fact
- Bank of England confirmed this explicitly in 2014 quarterly bulletin
- Private banks create the nation's money supply as interest-bearing debt
- The interest on that debt did not exist when the money was created
- System structurally requires continuous new borrowing to service existing debt
- Money to pay interest can only come from more debt creation

```
Borrow $100 at 10% interest
Must repay $110
The extra $10 doesn't exist yet
Must borrow again to get it
System requires perpetual expansion or it collapses
```

This is not a bug. It is the design.

### Step 2 — Cantillon Extraction

First receivers of new money buy assets before prices rise.
Last receivers (workers, savers) pay inflated prices.
Wealth transfers automatically with every monetary expansion — no legislation required.

### Step 3 — Interest as Permanent Tax on Productive Economy

Every business that borrows to invest pays interest to finance.
Every mortgage payer pays interest to bank.
Every government bond pays interest to bondholders.

Michael Hudson's calculation: **interest payments now exceed profits in the
productive economy.** Finance extracts more from production than production retains.

The productive economy works for the financial economy, not the other way around.

### Step 4 — Asset Inflation as Wealth Transfer

QE explicitly inflates asset prices:
- Top 1% own ~40% of financial assets
- Bottom 50% own ~2%
- Every QE round transfers purchasing power from non-asset holders to asset holders
- Monetary policy as class policy — dressed as technical necessity

### Step 5 — Privatise Gains, Socialise Losses

- 2008: banks created instruments they knew were fraudulent
- Goldman Sachs shorting CDOs they sold to clients — documented, settled, no prosecution
- When it collapsed: public bailout
- Heads I win, tails you lose
- The asymmetry is the point — not capitalism with risk, capitalism with guaranteed extraction

### Step 6 — Debt as Control Mechanism

- Indebted workers cannot strike — mortgage payments don't pause
- Indebted nations cannot pursue independent policy — IMF conditionality
- Indebted governments cannot invest — "markets" punish fiscal expansion
- Debt is political control dressed as financial obligation

## The Fraud Layer — Technically Legal, Functionally Deceptive

### Fractional Reserve Misrepresentation

The public is taught: banks take deposits and lend them out. This is false.

Banks create deposits when they lend. Your deposit is not lent to someone
else — it is a liability on the bank's balance sheet. The bank created new
money and owes you money simultaneously.

This misrepresentation frames banks as **intermediaries** (neutral, useful)
when they are actually **money creators** (powerful, extractive).

### Compound Interest

- $10,000 debt at 20% (credit card rate)
- Minimum payments only → 30+ years to repay
- Total paid: $80,000+
- Bank created the original $10,000 with a keystroke

### The Securitisation Scam — 2008

- Banks issued mortgages to people who couldn't repay
- Packaged into CDOs — obscured underlying risk
- Paid rating agencies to rate them AAA
- Sold to pension funds (workers' retirement savings)
- Took out credit default swaps betting they'd fail
- Collected when they failed
- Got bailed out when system imploded
- One mid-level trader went to jail — architects received bonuses

## The Legal Framework as Extraction Tool

### Property Law
- Land ownership = right to collect rent in perpetuity for doing nothing
- Land value created by community captured privately
- Planning/zoning restrictions maintained by landowners to restrict supply
- Henry George identified this in 1879 — nothing has changed

### Corporate Law
- Limited liability = socialise downside, privatise upside
- Shareholder primacy doctrine (Friedman 1970) — legally entrenched extraction
  from workers, suppliers, communities
- Share buybacks legal since SEC Rule 10b-18 (1982) — Reagan gift to finance

### Tax Law
- Capital gains taxed lower than labour income — no economic justification
- Carried interest — PE managers pay capital gains rates on labour income
- Step-up basis at death — unrealised gains never taxed if held until death
- Offshore structures — Cayman domicile eliminates tax entirely
- Every one of these written by people who benefit from it

### Bankruptcy Law
- Corporate bankruptcy protects shareholders while workers lose pensions
- Sears, Toys R Us: PE extracted billions in fees, loaded with debt, bankrupted
- Workers lost jobs and benefits — legally structured extraction

## The Ideological Cover

### "Efficient Markets"
- Eugene Fama's hypothesis: markets price all available information correctly
- Therefore current wealth distribution reflects underlying productivity
- Funded by and serves financial sector — provides academic legitimacy
- Challenges framed as "inefficient"

### "Trickle Down"
- Enrich the top, wealth flows down
- 50 years of evidence shows it doesn't
- Functioned as permission structure for extraction

### "There Is No Alternative" (TINA)
- Thatcher's phrase — made alternatives ideologically illegitimate
- Naturalises financial capitalism as the endpoint of human economic organisation
- Any alternative framed as dangerous utopianism

### Complexity as Deliberate Obscurantism
- Financial instruments deliberately complex — CDO-squared, synthetic derivatives
- Complexity prevents public understanding → prevents democratic accountability
- Greenspan: if you understood what he said, he misspoke

### "Risk and Return"
- Frames extraction as reward for bearing risk
- Financial sector systematically eliminates its own risk through bailouts,
  insurance, derivatives — while collecting the "risk premium"
- Socialised risk, privatised return — the premium without the exposure

## The Historical Pattern

Every major civilisation that allowed finance to dominate production declined:

| Civilisation | Peak | Financialization | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rome | Productive empire | Coin debasement, late empire finance | Collapse |
| Florence / Venice | Manufacturing dominance | Banking dominance | Relative decline |
| Dutch Republic | 17th C manufacturing | 18th C financial centre | Lost to industrial Britain |
| Britain | Industrial revolution leader | City of London dominance | Deindustrialised |
| USA | 20th C manufacturing | Post-1980 financialization | In progress |

The pattern is consistent: finance initially enables production, then extracts
from it, then hollows it out, then the system weakens.

## Financial Capitalism vs a Ponzi Scheme

The mechanism is identical. The scale, legal status, and political protection differ.

| Ponzi Scheme | Financial Capitalism |
|---|---|
| Illegal | Legal (by design) |
| Collapses inevitably | Bailed out repeatedly |
| Promoters prosecuted | Promoters promoted |
| Returns paid from new investors | Returns paid from new debtors |
| Finite | Infinite (so far) |

## The Political Stabilisation Mechanism

The system recruits its victims into defending it:

- Workers with pension funds in stock market defend asset price inflation
- Homeowners defend property values that price out their children
- Small business owners defend low corporate taxes that mainly benefit large corporations
- Voters support politicians funded by the system to protect the system

**Everyone with any asset has a small stake in the system continuing** — even
as the system primarily serves those at the very top.

Broad enough asset ownership to create political coalition for continuation.
Concentrated enough to ensure primary benefits flow upward.

## The Verdict

> A system where a small group controls the mechanism of money creation, uses
> that control to continuously transfer wealth from producers to themselves,
> writes the laws that make this legal, funds the ideology that makes it seem
> natural, and captures the political process that might change it —
> **is a scam at the systems level even if each individual transaction appears
> legitimate.**

The only question is whether the stabilisation mechanism holds long enough for
the Eastern industrial model to render it structurally uncompetitive — at which
point the scam becomes unsustainable not because it is exposed but because it
**no longer produces enough real wealth to extract from.**
