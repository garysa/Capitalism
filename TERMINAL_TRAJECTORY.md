# The Terminal Trajectory — Eastern De-dollarization and Western Decline

## The Thesis

If the East continues to trade away from the reserve currency and builds its
own financial system — and the West fails to reindustrialize and reduce its
financial sector's rent-extracting dominance — Western systemic decline is
not a possibility. It is a mathematical consequence of the current path.

---

## The Eastern Financial Architecture Being Built Now

This is not theoretical. It is happening.

### Payment Systems
- **CIPS** (Cross-Border Interbank Payment System) — China's SWIFT alternative
  - 1,400+ financial institutions in 110 countries
  - Processing volume growing ~25% annually
  - Russia forced onto it post-2022 — demonstrated it works under pressure
- **mBridge** — multi-CBDC platform (China, UAE, Thailand, Hong Kong, Saudi Arabia)
  - Designed explicitly for cross-border settlement without dollar intermediation
  - BIS involvement legitimises it — not a rogue system

### Settlement Currency Alternatives
- Commodity contracts increasingly priced in yuan, ruble, rupee bilaterally
- Russia selling energy in rubles and yuan
- Saudi Arabia accepting yuan for oil — partial but trend is clear
- Iran, Venezuela trading outside dollar for years — sanctions resistance proven
- If commodities price in multiple currencies, dollar loses structural necessity

### Development Finance
- **New Development Bank (BRICS)** — $33bn deployed, growing
- **Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB)** — 109 member countries
- Belt and Road financing — infrastructure loans creating yuan-denominated
  debt relationships across 140+ countries
- Alternatives to IMF/World Bank conditionality — no forced financialization

### Gold Accumulation
- China, Russia, India, Turkey all significantly increasing gold reserves
- China's official gold reserves likely understated — real figure estimated 3-5x official
- Gold as reserve asset removes dollar dependency
- Russia post-sanctions: gold holdings were unsanctionable — demonstrated viability

## The Tipping Point Mechanism

The dollar reserve system has a non-linear collapse risk that is widely
underappreciated:

```
Current state: Dollar used because everyone uses dollars
        ↓
Eastern alternative reaches critical mass of users
        ↓
Network effect shifts — each new user makes alternative more useful
        ↓
Marginal nations switch — those with least to lose from US relations
        ↓
Switching becomes rational for more nations
        ↓
Dollar demand falls → dollar weakens
        ↓
Weaker dollar makes dollar assets less attractive
        ↓
Further switching accelerates
        ↓
Non-linear collapse of reserve status
```

**The dangerous part:** This process is invisible until it isn't. Reserve
currency status looks stable right until the moment it breaks — like a bridge
that holds until it doesn't.

### Historical Precedent — Sterling

- British pound was global reserve currency
- Looked stable through 1930s-40s
- Bretton Woods (1944) formalised dollar replacement
- Sterling crisis 1967 — IMF bailout of the reserve currency issuer
- Happened faster than anyone predicted once momentum shifted

The US has one advantage Britain didn't — nuclear deterrence and military
dominance. But **you cannot bomb your way to reserve currency status** when
alternatives exist and trading partners choose them freely.

## What Dollar Reserve Loss Means for Western Living Standards

Not abstract — the consequences are concrete and severe:

### Loss of Seigniorage
- US issues dollars, world holds them as reserves
- World gives America real goods in exchange for printed paper
- Estimate: $500bn-$1 trillion per year in implicit subsidy
- Disappears as reserves diversify

### Loss of Deficit Financing
- US runs perpetual current account deficit — imports more than it exports
- Financed by world's willingness to hold dollar assets
- When that willingness declines, US must balance its trade
- Adjustment is brutal — import prices rise, living standards fall sharply
- Americans feel this immediately at the consumer level

### Loss of Sanctions Power
- Dollar reserve status = financial weapon
- Sanctioning nations requires them to use dollars
- As Eastern system grows, sanctions become less effective
- US loses its primary non-military geopolitical tool

### Interest Rate Consequences
- World currently absorbs US Treasury debt at relatively low rates
- As dollar reserve demand falls, rates must rise to attract buyers
- US debt servicing already at record highs on $35 trillion debt
- Higher rates = fiscal crisis without any external shock required

## Why the West Cannot Reduce Banking's Size Without Structural Crisis

### Finance Is Now Structurally Load-Bearing

The Western economy has been built around the financial sector for 50 years:
- UK: financial services ~12% of GDP, largest single sector
- US: finance + real estate ~20% of GDP
- Pension funds, insurance, mortgages — entire retirement and housing systems
  run through finance
- Shrinking finance means shrinking the systems ordinary people depend on

### You Cannot Shrink It Gradually Without Triggering the Crisis It Causes

- Reduce bank credit creation → credit crunch → recession
- Deflate asset prices deliberately → pension funds collapse → political crisis
- Tax financial transactions → capital flight → market disruption
- Each reform mechanism triggers the very instability it aims to prevent

### The Sector Defends Itself

- Volcker Rule (2010) — diluted before implementation
- Glass-Steagall restoration — never happened despite bipartisan rhetoric
- Financial transaction tax — killed in committee repeatedly
- Every serious attempt to reduce finance has been defeated through the
  mechanisms already documented (revolving door, lobbying, capture)

## The Self-Reinforcing Decline Loop

```
West financializes → productive capacity hollows out
        ↓
East industrializes → productive capacity grows
        ↓
East accumulates trade surpluses → buys fewer dollar assets
        ↓
East builds alternative financial system → dollar less necessary
        ↓
Dollar reserve status weakens → US financing costs rise
        ↓
West doubles down on finance (only sector generating returns)
        ↓
Further deindustrialization → further dependence on dollar privilege
        ↓
Dollar privilege eroding → crisis approaches
        ↓
No productive base to fall back on
```

Each step makes the next step worse. It is a self-reinforcing decline loop
with no internal correction mechanism.

The West is currently at the **"doubles down on finance"** stage.

## The Three Scenarios

### Scenario 1 — Managed Transition (Probability: ~5-10%)

West voluntarily:
- Implements serious industrial policy
- Reduces financial sector through gradual structural reform
- Accepts temporary reduction in living standards during transition
- Negotiates multipolar financial architecture

Requires political will that does not exist and vested interests that
would never permit it.

### Scenario 2 — Crisis-Forced Reform (Probability: ~30-40%)

A trigger event forces restructuring:
- Taiwan conflict disrupts supply chains catastrophically
- Dollar crisis acute enough to force emergency response
- Debt crisis makes financialization model visibly unsustainable
- AI-driven unemployment breaks the political coalition defending finance

West restructures under crisis pressure — painful but survivable.
Similar to New Deal moment. Requires the right political leadership
at the right moment.

### Scenario 3 — Gradual Decline to Secondary Status (Probability: ~50-60%)

- No decisive crisis, no decisive reform
- Slow erosion of dollar privilege over 20-30 years
- Western living standards gradually decline relative to East
- Periodic financial crises each partially addressed
- East becomes dominant productive and increasingly financial power
- West retains relevance but loses primacy

**This is the default trajectory if nothing changes.**

## What Reform Would Actually Require

For the West to avoid Scenario 3:

**Reindustrialization**
- Sustained industrial policy — CHIPS/IRA scale applied across sectors
- State-directed patient capital for strategic industries
- Accepting short-term financial returns for long-term productive capacity
- 20-30 year commitment immune to electoral cycles

**Financial Sector Reduction**
- Restore functional separation of commercial and investment banking
- Financial transaction tax to reduce high-frequency rent extraction
- Break up Too Big To Fail institutions
- End carried interest, step-up basis, offshore structuring

**Monetary Reform**
- Public money creation — remove private bank monopoly on money
- Sovereign wealth fund — redirect surplus into productive investment
- Remove perverse QE incentives that inflate assets over production

**Political Reform**
- Campaign finance — remove financial sector from political funding
- Revolving door restrictions — genuine cooling off periods
- Lobbying transparency and limits
- Without this, all other reforms are captured before implementation

Each of these is individually politically near-impossible.
Together they represent a systemic transformation that has no historical
precedent without a preceding crisis.

## The Deepest Irony

Financial capitalism was sold as the highest form of economic organisation —
the endpoint of history. It turns out it was a 40-year extraction operation
that consumed the productive capacity that made the West dominant, while the
East watched, learned from the mistake, and built something more durable.

The Eastern model wins not because it is more just or more free.
It wins because **it did not allow finance to consume production.**

That single structural difference, maintained over decades, is sufficient
to determine the outcome — unless the West rediscovers that
**wealth is made, not financialized.**
