# Capitalism: Eastern Ascendancy vs Western Decline

An analysis of financial capitalism, industrial capitalism, and the structural
forces driving Eastern model ascendancy over the Western financialized system.

---

## Core Thesis

The West has shifted from industrial capitalism (capital deployed to produce
goods) toward financial capitalism (capital deployed to extract rent). China and
the broader Eastern bloc have maintained political primacy over capital,
deploying it into productive industrial capacity at speed and scale the West
cannot currently match.

---

## The Two Models

### Western Financial Capitalism
- Financialization of the economy since 1970s-80s
- Finance sector: ~10% of US corporate profits (1980) → ~40% (2024)
- Short-termism — quarterly earnings over long-term investment
- Rent-seeking over value creation
- Political capture by financial interests prevents structural reform

### Eastern Industrial / Developmental State Capitalism
- State-directed long-horizon industrial policy
- 5-year plans enabling sustained strategic investment
- Political primacy over capital (Xi crushing Jack Ma as symbol)
- Manufacturing dominance: solar, EVs, batteries, shipbuilding, rail
- China ~30% of global manufacturing output

---

## The Dollar System Under Pressure

- US debt-to-GDP ~125% and accelerating
- Dollar share of global reserves: ~70% (2000) → ~58% (2024)
- BRICS building alternative payment rails
- Saudi Arabia accepting yuan for oil — cracks in petrodollar
- Interest payments on US debt now exceed defence spending
- Slow structural decline rather than acute collapse

---

## Financial Capitalism as Rent-Seeking

The FIRE economy (Finance, Insurance, Real Estate) extracts value from future
production and concentrates it among asset holders:

- Most financial products are zero-sum redistribution, not wealth creation
- High-frequency trading extracts without contributing to price discovery
- Private equity extracts via leverage and fees
- Share buybacks over capital investment
- Carried interest tax treatment — political capture codified in law

---

## Can the West Reform Without Collapse?

Historical pattern: major structural reform of capitalism has almost always
required crisis, war, or near-collapse to overcome incumbent resistance.

**The 2008 test case:** The perfect reform moment — yet:
- Banks bailed out, not restructured
- QE inflated asset prices — opposite of redistribution
- Finance sector larger and more concentrated within 5 years
- Demonstrated the bailout reflex is stronger than reform impulse

**Most likely path:** Managed decline punctuated by crises, each partially
addressed — slow suffocation rather than dramatic collapse.

## Vested Interests Blocking Western Reform

### Financial Sector Core
- Major banks (Too Big To Fail) — JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, Citigroup
- Asset management — BlackRock (~$10 trillion AUM), Vanguard, State Street
- Hedge funds and private equity — Blackstone, KKR, Carlyle
- Benefit from implicit government guarantee and QE-driven asset inflation

### The Revolving Door (Key Mechanism)
- Treasury → Goldman Sachs → Treasury (Rubin, Paulson, Mnuchin pattern)
- Regulators drawn from and return to industry they regulate
- Brooksley Born blocked from regulating derivatives (1996-99) by:
  - Greenspan (Fed), Rubin (Treasury), Summers (Deputy Treasury), Levitt (SEC)
- People writing rules are people who benefit from them

### Political Capture
- Gilens & Page (2014) Princeton study: elite preferences prevail ~100% of the
  time when conflicting with public preferences on economic policy
- US lobbying spend: ~$4 billion/year officially
- Citizens United (2010) removed corporate political spending limits
- Both parties receive substantial finance sector funding — structural capture

### Think Tank / Intellectual Infrastructure
- Cato Institute, Heritage Foundation — Koch network and finance sector funded
- Peterson Institute — Pete Peterson (Blackstone) spent ~$500m on deficit
  hawkery that blocks public investment
- Brookings — "centrist" cover for status quo
- Economics journals — heterodox approaches systematically excluded

### International Blocking Mechanisms
- IMF conditionality — exports financial capitalism model to developing world
- World Bank — currently led by ex-Mastercard CEO
- WTO rules written by US/EU corporate lawyers
- SWIFT — dollar payment infrastructure weaponised as geopolitical tool

### China
- **Xi Jinping** — consolidated power sufficient to override financial interests,
  long time horizon, Made in China 2025, Belt and Road, Common Prosperity
- **Wang Qishan** — anti-corruption campaign eliminated Western-style capture
  mechanism before it fully took hold

  Washington Consensus

  the financialized West cannot replicate

### Russia
- **Elvira Nabiullina** — stabilised ruble post-2022 sanctions, proved dollar

### Gulf States

  petrodollar stability

  multi-currency settlement

### Multilateral
- **Dilma Rousseff (NDB)** — BRICS development bank, non-dollar alternative
  to World Bank
- **Sergei Glazyev (EEU)** — Greater Eurasian Partnership theoretical framework

## The Crypto Question

### What Crypto Gets Right
- Trustless settlement without intermediaries
- Permissionless access
- Transparent ledgers vs opaque financial system
- Censorship resistance

### What Crypto Hasn't Solved
- Scalability — Bitcoin ~7 tx/sec vs Visa ~24,000
- Volatility — cannot function as unit of account for industrial planning
- Governance — decentralized systems recentralize around developers/whales
- Capture — BlackRock Bitcoin ETF is Wall St absorbing crypto, not being
  displaced by it

### Most Plausible Role
Transitional infrastructure — enabling non-dollar settlement between nations.
The new system doesn't defeat the old one, it makes it obsolete.

## Structural Contrast

| Western Blockers | Eastern Drivers |
|---|---|
| Protect existing rent extraction | Build new productive capacity |
| 2-4 year time horizons | 10-30 year time horizons |
| Financiers and lobbyists | Engineers and planners |
| React to markets | Shape markets |
| Financial returns over production | Productive output over financial returns |

---

## Core Conclusion

> The Eastern model is ascending not because it found a better form of
> capitalism but because it maintained **political primacy over capital** —
> the thing Western democracies surrendered to financial interests over the
> past 50 years.

The West has the knowledge, technology, and resources to reform. What it lacks
is a political mechanism to overcome vested interests — and historically that
mechanism has almost always been crisis, war, or collapse.

The most likely scenario: **the West begins reindustrialization but too slowly,
too incoherently, and too captured by financial interests to close the
competitive gap before Eastern model dominance becomes structural.**
